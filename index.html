Killarney → Killorglin

Friday, May 29
23 km
↑ 64m   Flat

The Ride

Morning train from Dublin Heuston (3h 15m). Pick up bikes in Killarney. Easy afternoon spin along the River Laune — flat, quiet roads, the perfect warm-up.

Don't Miss

  • River Laune — salmon fishing country, lovely riverside cycling
  • Killorglin town — a real working town, not a tourist trap

Elevation Profile — 23 km

Tonight in Killorglin

Laune Bridge House B&B — triple room, 3-minute walk to town. Book direct or Booking.com

Eat: Nick's Seafood & Gastro Bar or Kingdom 1795 for dinner. Pint at Falvey's — you might catch a trad session.

2

Killorglin → Cahersiveen

Saturday, May 30
47 km
↑ 281m   Moderate

The Ride

Your first proper day. Through the village of Glenbeigh, then the famous Mountain Stage — a coastal road carved into the cliffs above Dingle Bay. The first time you see the Atlantic will stop you in your tracks. Rolling terrain with one solid climb before Mountain Stage.

Don't Miss

  • Rossbeigh Beach — 7km Blue Flag beach, short detour from Glenbeigh
  • Mountain Stage — epic cliff road with views across to the Dingle Peninsula
  • Cahersiveen's colourful main street

Tonight in Cahersiveen

Cúl Draíochta B&B — sea views, cycling-friendly hosts who'll dry your gear. Booking.com

Eat: QC's for top-tier fresh seafood. Pint at Mike Murt's — old-school pub with trad sessions.

Cahersiveen → Caherdaniel via Skellig Ring

Sunday, May 31
52 km
↑ 347m   Moderate
© geograph.org.uk CC-BY-SA

The Ride

Neil's key upgrade: instead of the direct N70, take the Skellig Ring coastal road — the scenic loop via Portmagee with "siiiick views" (Neil's words). Through the colourful fishing village of Portmagee, past the Kerry Cliffs (over 300m — higher than the Cliffs of Moher), with views out to the Skellig Islands. Rejoin the N70 at Waterville, then over Coomakista Pass (215m, 5.8km at 3%) for a panoramic descent into Caherdaniel.

Don't Miss

  • Skellig Ring coastal road — the views Neil specifically flagged
  • Portmagee — colourful fishing village, great coffee stop
  • Kerry Cliffs — 300m drop, views of Skellig Michael (UNESCO site)
  • Coomakista Pass — panoramic Atlantic views on the descent
  • Derrynane Beach — "incredible" (per Neil). Blue Flag, white sand, sheltered bay

Elevation Profile — 52 km — Neil says: take the coastal road, "siiiick views"

Tonight in Caherdaniel

Tobervilla Guest House — Deluxe Triple Room in a restored historic building near Derrynane Beach. Book direct

Eat: The Blind Piper — hearty locally-sourced food, proper character pub. Pint at Keating's for the best Guinness in Kerry.

4

Caherdaniel → Kenmare

Monday, June 1
45 km
↑ 260m   Easy

The Ride

The recovery day. A gentle ride along the south coast of the Iveragh Peninsula, skirting Kenmare Bay with views across to the Beara Peninsula. The shortest and easiest day — save your legs, because tomorrow is the big one. Stop in Sneem — one of Ireland's most colourful villages — for lunch.

Don't Miss

  • Sneem — two village squares, brightly painted houses, great lunch stop
  • Parknasilla — woodland, inlets, hidden beaches along the coast road
  • Kenmare Bay — views of the Beara Peninsula and MacGillycuddy's Reeks

Elevation Profile — 45 km (validated via BRouter cycling router, N70 coast road)

Tonight in Kenmare

Foley's Town House — heart of Kenmare with its own restaurant, bar, and live trad music. Book direct or Booking.com

Eat: Foley's own restaurant or explore Kenmare's many options. This is a proper food town — you won't go wrong.

5

Kenmare → Killarney via Gap of Dunloe ⭐

Tuesday, June 2 — The Savage Day
~55 km
↑ ~760m   Hard
© Wikimedia Commons CC-BY-SA

The Ride

This is what you came for. Climb to Moll's Gap (262m) — the highest point on the Ring of Kerry — then descend into the remote Black Valley, one of the last places in Ireland to get electricity. From there, climb again through the narrow, magical Gap of Dunloe (241m), past five lakes and the Wishing Bridge, before a glorious descent to Kate Kearney's Cottage and on to Killarney.

Don't Miss

  • Moll's Gap — panoramic views of Upper Lake and MacGillycuddy's Reeks
  • Ladies View — Queen Victoria's ladies-in-waiting loved this viewpoint in 1861
  • Black Valley — remote, silent, spectacular glacial valley
  • Gap of Dunloe — narrow pass between Purple Mountain and the Reeks, five lakes
  • Kate Kearney's Cottage — traditional pub at the foot of the Gap. You've earned it.

Elevation Profile — ~55 km — "The route is savage" — Neil's mate (Gap of Dunloe road not in routing APIs; distance from kerrycycling.com + Kenmare–Moll's Gap segment validated via BRouter)

Tonight in Killarney — Celebration Dinner

Killarney Lodge — 4-star guesthouse, 1 minute walk from the train station. Book direct or Booking.com

Eat: You've just cycled through the best scenery in Ireland. Treat yourselves. Killarney has endless options. Return bikes to Killarney Bike Rental.

Wednesday, June 3

Train to Dublin

Morning train Killarney → Dublin Heuston (~3h 15m). Killarney Lodge is a 1-minute walk from the station. Night in Dublin — no bikes, no hills, just pubs.

Thursday, June 4

Fly Home

Relaxed day in Dublin. No stress. Flight home with five days of Kerry scenery burned into your memory.
